A Comprehensive Benchmark for EvaluatingText-to-Audio Models

PAPER GITHUB DATASET

Text-to-Audio (TTA) generation has made significant strides with the rise of large-scale generative models. However, existing evaluation methods for TTA systems are often fragmented, covering only limited aspects of model performance and relying heavily on manual assessment. To address this, we propose AudioEval, a comprehensive and standardized benchmark designed to systematically evaluate TTA models across multiple dimensions including accuracy, generalization, efficiency, reliability, and fairness. AudioEval encompasses ten state-of-the-art TTA models, combining large-scale automatic evaluation metrics with human annotations to ensure robustness and reproducibility. Our experimental results demonstrate that AudioEval effectively differentiates model capabilities at various granularities and provides actionable insights for improving TTA systems.

We identify 5 different aspects that are important in real-world model deployment, including:

  • accuracy
  • generalization
  • efficiency
  • bias
  • fairness
  • toxicity
  • robustness

By curating scenarios encompassing these aspects, we evaluate state-of-the-art text-to-image models using this benchmark. Unlike previous evaluations that focused on alignment and quality, HEIM significantly improves coverage by evaluating all models across all aspects. Our results reveal that no single model excels in all aspects, with different models demonstrating strengths in different aspects.

For full transparency, this website contains all the prompts, generated audios and the results for the automated and human evaluation metrics.
